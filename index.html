HAT OVER HATE

Not a movement—just an additive. Supporting Whatcom County one hand-painted conversation at a time.

Hear My Heart

THE STORY

Hey, I'm Tana. I'm an artist in Ferndale, and I believe that if we can just find community and care about each other, we can stop the polarization.

I can't donate money to the movements already doing good work in Whatcom County, but I can paint. I believe Hat over Hate is a way for me to lend myself to the issues of today—to the butterfly effect of a single conversation.

"It’s about going out, having those difficult conversations, and being the one who listens."

I'm not trying to start a movement—I'm trying to be an additive to the ones already running. It's about finding common ground so we can help our children and our community.

WHY CONVERSATION?

STUBBORNNESS VS. LISTENING

People are so divided now, just trying to prove their point or sticking to their pride. I enjoy hearing both sides because I know beliefs have a source—families, grandmas, history.

REALIZING THE TRUTH

I had to realize things on my own, not because someone taught me, but because someone listened to me. That's the power I want to share.

KNOWING OUR NEIGHBORS

It’s hard for me to talk on the phone, even to family. It's hard to acknowledge neighbors beyond a wave. But I worry for them every day. We need to get back to truly talking.

THE POWER OF THE "LITTLE VOTE"

I didn't care about civics until the world started to change. We need realistic education. We need to understand how the little votes turn into the bigger ones that change our world.
