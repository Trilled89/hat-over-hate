The Story

Hey, I'm Tana. I'm an artist in Ferndale, and I’ve reached a point where I can’t just sit and watch the polarization anymore. I believe that if we can just find community and actually care about each other again, we can stop the bleeding.

I don't have a massive bank account to donate to the movements already doing the heavy lifting in Whatcom County, but I have my hands and I have paint. I'm not trying to start something new—I'm trying to be a catalyst for the good things already running.

“It's about going out, having those difficult conversations, and being the one who finally listens.”
Philosophy

Stubbornness vs. Listening

We all know where our opinions come from. They come from our families, our grandmas, our history, and the things that kept us safe. I’ve realized that I actually enjoy hearing both sides, because every "stubborn" belief has a human source. If we don't listen, we're just shouting at ghosts.

Realizing the Truth

I didn't change my mind because someone lectured me or called me names. I changed my thinking because I finally met someone who listened to me first. Once I felt heard, my guard dropped, and I finally wanted to understand them too. That’s the only way the truth actually sticks—through a connection, not a correction.
