Hats over Hate

Turning hand-painted hats into bridges of conversation. Building a more connected Ferndale, one dialogue at a time.

View the Process

A simple idea for complex times.

I'm Tana. Like many of you, I'm tired of feeling helpless while the world feels more polarized every day. I believe the solution isn't in big gestures, but in finding community, caring for our neighbors, and rediscovering how to talk to one another with respect.

"If we can find common ground, we can find a way forward. It starts with a conversation."

I don't have a lot of money to donate to causes, but I have a brush and a belief that a hat can be more than an accessory—it can be a conversation piece that actually matters.

01

Hand-Painted Art

Every hat is personally hand-painted in my Ferndale studio. Each design is a unique invitation to engage with the world.

02

Prompted Dialogue

Hidden inside the front of each hat are thoughtful questions designed to veer any conversation toward things that actually matter.

03

The Rules of Engagement

On every tag and receipt, you'll find a set of 'Conversation Rules'—simple tips for maintaining calmness, openness, and curiosity.

Inside the Brim

  • What is something that matters to your community?
  • What is a local issue you'd like to solve?
  • How can we better support our neighbors today?

The Rules

  • Listen with the intent to understand.
  • Maintain a spirit of calm curiosity.
  • Seek what connects us, not what divides.
  • Respect the person behind the opinion.

Ferndale Focus

I want to work with community programs and local businesses in Ferndale, WA that are already helping. I believe our high schoolers deserve a better education in civics and local elections—knowing how to vote and how to participate in our local community is the most powerful tool we have.

Let's build something that matters, right here at home.
